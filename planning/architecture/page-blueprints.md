## `/` — Home

**Purpose:** First impression. Communicate the mission in seconds. Route every audience to where they need to go.

---

### Section 1 — Hero

Full-width section at the top of the page. Visual treatment: deep brand blue background with white text, or cream with blue treatment.

**Headline:** Mission statement, direct and leading — *"Promoting lifelong wellbeing through experiential learning."*

**Sub-copy:** One or two sentences on who Empowr is and who they serve. The all-ages signal must appear here — this is a key differentiator. The current site reads as a skating club; the new site must read as a wellbeing organisation from the first line.

**CTAs (two buttons):**
- Primary: Find Out More → `/about`
- Secondary: Support Our Work → Empowr Heroes platform

**Improvement over current site:** The current site opens with skating activities. This hero leads with the mission — activities appear later as evidence of the mission, not as the product being sold.

---

### Section 2 — What We Stand For

Three or four belief pillars laid out as cards or columns. Each pillar is not just a word — it is a short statement in Empowr's voice.

Example structure (copy to be confirmed):
- **Wellbeing** — *"We believe everyone deserves the tools to live well."*
- **Learning** — *"We believe experience is the most powerful teacher."*
- **Community** — *"We believe change happens when people come together."*
- **Inclusion** — *"We believe wellbeing is for everyone, at every age."*

These anchor the narrative immediately for any visitor.

---

### Section 3 — Who We Work With

Short copy block or simple visual making the all-ages message explicit. This challenges the assumption that Empowr is a youth organisation.

Content: children, young people, adults, older adults, families, communities.

Keep this brief — it supports the pillars rather than expanding on them. The `/our-work` page carries the detail.

---

### Section 4 — Get Involved Routes

Four cards, one per audience route. Each card: heading, one-line description, single CTA button.

| Card | Description | Button | Destination |

### Section 5 — Latest News

Strip at the bottom of the page showing two or three most recent news posts. Each: title, date, short excerpt, Read More link.

Keeps the site feeling alive and shows Empowr is active.

**Note:** The current site's last news post is from January 2023 — over three years ago. Launching the new site with at least one current news post is important. A relaunch announcement would serve this purpose and signal that Empowr is active again.

## `/about` — Who We Are

**Purpose:** Build trust and understanding. Explain what Empowr is, why it exists, and what makes it distinctive.

---

### Section 1 — Page Header

Headline: *"Who We Are"*

One paragraph intro on Empowr's purpose — expanding on the mission statement in the user's own voice.

---

### Section 2 — Our Story

Two or three paragraphs of founding narrative. The core hook is already confirmed and is strong: the founder noticed there were no indoor roller skating venues in SE London. Rather than just opening a rink, they used that gap to launch a community wellbeing programme in February 2022. Skating was the entry point — wellbeing through experiential learning was always the mission.

This is a specific, human, credible origin story and should be told exactly like that. Not corporate, not sector-language. The skating origin makes the organisation memorable and distinguishes it from generic wellbeing providers.

*The hook above is confirmed from the current site. Needs expanding to 2–3 paragraphs in Empowr's voice — the detail and tone to come from the founder.*

---

### Section 3 — What Is a CIC?

Plain-language explainer for anyone unfamiliar with the Community Interest Company structure. Key points:
- A CIC is a type of company designed to benefit the community
- Profits are reinvested into the community, not distributed to shareholders
- It is a regulated legal structure with annual reporting requirements
- Annual reports (CIC 34) are publicly filed and available to view

Closes with a link to the CIC Regulator website for anyone who wants to verify or learn more.

This section matters most to funders and partners who need to understand the governance model and assess credibility.

## `/our-work` — What We Do

**Purpose:** Make the work concrete. Show what experiential learning looks like in practice and who it reaches.

---

### Section 1 — Page Header

Headline: *"What We Do"*

Opening paragraph expanding on the mission — what experiential learning means in Empowr's context.

---

### Section 2 — Our Approach

The methodology — now fully confirmed from Notion. Key points to cover:
- Empowr designs and delivers programmes through **EELA (Empowr Experiential Learning Activities)** — a structured framework ensuring every session is grounded in psychological safety, inclusion, meaningful challenge, and joy
- Facilitators are trained through **ECCP (Empowr Certified Coaching Program)** — training people to hold space and lead with empathy, not just instruct
- The science: experiential learning (Dewey, Kolb) creates new neural pathways, improves brain function, reduces stress hormones — physical activity and mental stimulation combined
- Skating (and other activities) is the *vehicle*; wellbeing and learning are the *destination*

Full source copy in `planning/architecture/narrative.md` — Our Approach and Our Why sections.

### Section 4 — Programmes

Named programmes displayed as cards. Per card: name, short description, who it is for, status (Active / Past).

**Framing note:** Past programmes must not be hidden or omitted. They are evidence that Empowr is not a skating club — bouldering, digital art, coding, hula hoop, and zumba demonstrate that skating is one vehicle among many. Past programmes should appear with a "Past Programme" label alongside active ones. This is a deliberate narrative choice to show breadth.

**Confirmed active programmes** (sourced from empowrcic.org/book-online):

|---|---|---|

| SYNKRON8 | 15+ | Structured session focused on routines and linking moves |

| All Ages Roller Disco | 5+ | Community skating session |

3. **Courses** — Beginners, Foundations, Prep to Street
4. **Camps** — Quad Camp, Roller Camp (holiday programmes)
5. **Advanced** — SYNKRON8

**Still needed:** 2–3 sentence descriptions for each group (what participants experience, what they gain). Link to booking: empowrcic.org/book-online.

**Past programmes** (show breadth — these are not dead ends, they are evidence of the methodology applied to different vehicles):
