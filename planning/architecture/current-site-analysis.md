## What the current site does well

- The mission statement exists: *"empowering lives by promoting holistic well-being through the transformative power of experiential learning"*
- Community feel — the WhatsApp group link shows organic community engagement
- Honest about being a community-first organisation that keeps fees low and runs free events
- Has documented real partnerships and milestones in the news posts
- Has a functioning donate link

---

## What the current site does poorly — and how the new site fixes it

### 1. Activities lead, mission is buried

**Problem:** The current site is dominated by roller skating. Visitors who don't already know Empowr will assume it is a skating club, not a wellbeing CIC. The mission statement exists but is not prominent.

**Fix:** The new site leads with the mission in the hero section. Programmes (skating and others) are presented as *the vehicle for delivering wellbeing and experiential learning*, not as the product itself. The homepage positions activities as evidence of the mission, not the other way around.

---

### 2. No narrative architecture

**Problem:** There is no clear story on the current site — why Empowr was founded, what drives it, what it believes. The "Who We Are" page redirected to a donation/impact page in testing, suggesting the about content either doesn't exist or isn't properly structured.

**Fix:** The new `/about` page carries the founding story (SE London, 2022, skating as the entry point), values, and a clear explanation of the CIC structure. The narrative is explicit and intentional.

---

### 3. No CIC transparency

**Problem:** The CIC structure is mentioned in the footer (company number, registered address) but the site does not explain what a CIC is, why it matters, or link to the annual reports. For funders and partners, this is a missed opportunity — the CIC structure is a trust signal, not just a legal formality.

**Fix:** The new `/about` page includes a plain-language CIC explainer. The new `/impact` page lists every CIC 34 annual report with a direct link to the CIC Regulator filing. This turns compliance into credibility.

---

### 4. No audience segmentation

**Problem:** There is no clear path for funders, partners, or freelancers. Everyone lands on the same homepage and either finds their way or doesn't. The primary CTA for the community is a WhatsApp link — which is informal and not appropriate as the main engagement route for a credibility-focused site.

**Fix:** The new homepage has four explicit audience route cards. The `/get-involved` page forks clearly between donors, partners, practitioners, and community members. The WhatsApp link can still exist (under Join the Community) but is not a primary CTA.

---

### 5. Breadth of work is invisible

**Problem:** The past programmes (bouldering, hula hoop, digital art, coding, zumba) are listed on the current site but not framed as evidence of the mission. They look like abandoned activities rather than proof that Empowr uses many vehicles to deliver wellbeing and learning.

**Fix:** The new `/our-work` page contextualises programmes within the approach — experiential learning through different activities. Past programmes become part of the story, not a list of things that stopped.

---

### 6. Stale content

**Problem:** No news since January 2023. For anyone assessing Empowr's credibility — funders, partners, press — a three-year content gap is a red flag.

**Fix:** The new site launches with at least one news post. The MDX-based news system makes it easy to publish without a CMS. Establishing a regular cadence (even one post per quarter) keeps the site credible.

---

### 7. No clear contact structure

**Problem:** The contact page was not reachable via the standard URL pattern. There is no obvious breakdown of who to contact for what.

**Fix:** The new `/contact` page has a structured form with a subject dropdown (General / Partnership / Media / Freelance / Other) and is clearly linked from every page.

## Notes for copywriting

- The founding story has a strong hook: the founder noticed there were no indoor roller skating venues in SE London, and used that gap to launch a community wellbeing programme. This is a human, specific, credible origin story.
- The 12-week Street Skate Support Group averaged 50+ skaters per week — this is a real impact number worth surfacing on the `/impact` page.
- The anniversary celebration was free of charge — this signals the community-first ethos clearly.
- "Welcoming and encouraging especially as a beginner" (testimonial) — the tone of accessibility and inclusion is genuine and should inform the voice of the new site.
