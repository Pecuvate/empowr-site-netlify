Our Mission

We exist on a simple belief

That doing things — moving, learning, connecting with others — is the most powerful way a person can improve their own health and well-being.

Not therapy. Not prescription. Not watching from the side lines. Doing.

Empowr CIC was founded in South East London in October 2021 by Jasmine and Shaun Barnett, starting with a single skating session and a belief that physical, social experience is transformative. We have grown to deliver over 10,000 participant attendances annually — across community sessions, school programmes, holiday camps, and events — reaching children, young people, and adults across our community.

Experiential learning is our method. Lifelong well-being is our mission. Skating — for now — is our medium.

The framework we have built is designed for far more than one activity. It is an infrastructure for community wellbeing — and it is growing.

The Need

A health crisis that prescriptions alone cannot solve

The UK is facing compounding physical and mental health challenges. One in four adults experiences a mental health problem each year. Physical inactivity costs the NHS an estimated £7.4 billion annually. Children and young people from lower-income backgrounds are disproportionately affected. And community connection — one of the most powerful protective factors for long-term mental health — is in decline.

Waiting lists treat the symptom. Empowr addresses the root.

When a person engages in a new physical, social experience — especially one that presents a manageable challenge — the brain forms new neural connections. Chronic stress, which accelerates cellular aging and weakens immunity, is reduced. Movement activates the body’s fascia system, supporting whole-body health. And shared activity creates belonging, accountability, and lasting wellbeing habits.

“Investing in experiential learning is not just a cultural or educational decision — it’s a smart, future-focused economic strategy.”

Empowr makes this case to commissioners, funders, and government — and our evidence is growing every year.

How We Work

Three engines. One system.

Empowr operates through three connected engines that compound on each other over time.

🏟

The Delivery Engine

We find and partner with venues, run skating sessions, workshops, lessons, events, and holiday camps, and deliver these through trained coaches and facilitators. This is what the community sees — but it is not the whole picture.

🎓

The Coach Development Engine

We recruit and train participants, volunteers, and community members through our Empowr Certified Coaching Programme (ECCP), creating pathways from participant to certified coach to programme leader. Our real asset is the growing network of people who can deliver Empowr experiences safely and sustainably.

🔗

The Support and Funding Engine

We generate income through commissioned delivery, government programmes, and participant-paid sessions. Alongside this, we seek funding and supporter investment — not to fund individual sessions, but to strengthen the whole system: more coaches, more spaces, more subsidised places.

The result is a flywheel:

1
Empowr secures support
2
Support funds capacity — coaches, venues, equipment
3
Capacity creates more sessions and more access
4
Sessions produce wellbeing outcomes and community trust
5
Outcomes produce evidence and public demand
6
Evidence attracts more support and partnerships
7
The system expands — more coaches, more locations, more lives

Where We’re Going

Building a Health Activities Provider for the UK

We are building towards becoming a recognised Health Activities Provider in the UK — a legitimate health intervention that sits alongside clinical care.

The long-term vision: GPs and social prescribers referring people to Empowr sessions the way they refer people to physiotherapy or talking therapies. Social prescribing is already NHS policy. Our task is to be ready when that pathway expands.

In the near term

ECCP launch — Our Empowr Certified Coaching Programme will convert participants into certified coaches, multiplying delivery capacity without proportionally increasing overhead. This is the infrastructure that makes national scale possible.

EELA expansion — Beyond our current skating programmes (MoveWell), our EELA framework supports five activity types: Movement & Wellness, Mind Body & Wellness, Creative Expression & Arts, Outdoor & Adventure, and Team-Building & Leadership. We are actively building the teams and partnerships to activate these.

Delivery hubs — Establishing permanent Empowr activity spaces in SE London and beyond, reducing venue dependency and increasing community access.

Evidence framework — Building a formal outcomes measurement system to support Social Return on Investment (SROI) reporting, enabling commissioner-grade funding applications and long-term government partnerships.
